Fried Rice

Description

I know it's everyone's least favorite part of the recipe. Mine too. So I'll just tell you a few tips before cutting to the case.

  • Wash your eggs before you break them, not before you put them into the fridge. That way they are going to stay fresh longer.
  • Don't forget to cook your rice beforehand.
  • White-parts are not actually white. They are allowed to be kinda green-ish. So the better name would be a solid part. The hollow part are the ones for decoration.
  • Also be aware that recipe is a lie and if I were you I would definitely dabble the amount.

Ingredients

Leftover rice
2 bowls
It would be perfect, if it spent a night in the fridge. But if your are not that good with planning - I sure am not - just make sure it's not right from the stove. Give your rice some time to cool down.
Green Onion
1 onion
But I don't think that they sell it like this, so you would still buy a ~50g pack.
Oil
1 tbs
Neutral flavor
Eggs
Actually just 1 large egg
If you are not sure are those large eggs or not, just use two.
Rice vinegar
1/2 tbs
Don't overdo this one otherwise... Wll, vinegar is not for everyone, right? You don't want you rice to be vinegar flavoured
Spice
For your taste
Not necessary, but highly recommended

Steps

  1. Beaten The Eggs
    • Or the egg. Just make them (it) fluffy

  2. (Under)Cook The Eggs
    • Stir-fry the eggs (or egg), but don't make an omelette. It still should be a little bit runny.

  3. Cut the onions
    • Separate the white-parts from the green-parts. It's kinda tricky part, but, if you read a description for you it would be easy-peasy-lemon-squeezy.

  4. Stir-fry the onions
    • Pretty self-explanatory for my taste. But remember we are only frying white-parts!

  5. Add rice
    • Important. We are making a fried rice not a mushed rice. So, yeah, it's a big chunk of rice, but be gentle separating it, okay?

  6. Add eggs
    • You will need to cut them into a smaller chunks with a help of a spatula, but be gentle with a rice.

  7. Add soy sauce and rice vinegar
    • Mix them good, but, yeah, I will say that again, be gentle with a rice.

  8. Add green-parts
    • Stir-fry them for a little bit more.

  9. Eat
    • No, seriously it's delicious.
